Why Sleep Matters

Sleep is essential for maintaining both physical and mental health. During sleep, your body repairs itself, strengthens your immune system, and processes information from the day.

Not getting enough sleep can lead to poor concentration, mood swings, and long-term health risks such as heart disease and weakened immunity.

Most people need 7–9 hours of sleep per night. Creating a regular sleep routine, avoiding screens before bed, and maintaining a calm environment can greatly improve sleep quality.

Better sleep leads to better focus, better health, and a better life.
